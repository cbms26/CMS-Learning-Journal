Joomla and WordPress

Last week, we learnt about Joomla CMS and also developed startup business website using Joomla features. However, WordPress offers different set of advantages and functionalities. As for me, installing Joomla to my local environment was challenging compared to WordPress. Joomla required XAMPP Cpanel to be installed and initiated, before installing itself whereas WordPress was easily installed to my local enviroment by Local by Flywheel software.
Futhermore, Joomla site structure was more complex and hard to navigate certain components. On the otherhand, I found WordPress more user-friendly and easy to use. I could smoothly navigate myself between pages, header, footer and other attributes of website any time for editing my website.

Working with WordPress

The difficulty that I experienced while working with Joomla made me appreciate WordPress even more. It was easy to install, easy to manage, and more easy to swap between site and site admin, which was indeed a more of CMS tool compared to Joomla. Nonetheless, Joomla CMS is meant for professional developers working on complex sites.
I found out about Gutenberg (Block Editor) feature offered by WordPress, which allowed to easily add, arrange and style web contents which made editing/managing content more efficient.
